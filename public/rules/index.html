Colony Grid

Rules and Setup

A social deduction in a broken network. Players jack anonymous programs into each other, the Terminal resolves them through the Grid, and hidden faction pressure decides who controls the system.

Setup

Recommended players: 8-10

Minimum: 6

Maximum: 12

Roles: Use the role pool generated for the session.

Physical materials: one player registration card and one power card for each player.

Terminal: the shared public machine where all programs are run.

Key Terms

Executor: the player currently running a program at the Terminal.

Source: the player who owns the scanned power card.

Target: the player chosen to be affected by that program.

Architect: a hidden role state. A player learns they are currently an Architect when the Terminal tells them after a run, or when another power reveals it.

Grid effect: an additional persistent rule installed by an Architect.

Boot Sequence

  1. Host creates a session and prints the registration cards, power cards, and role reference sheet.
  2. Each player takes one registration card and its matching power card.
  3. Each player opens the registration page, enters a handle, and scans their registration QR.
  4. The Grid privately reveals that player's faction and role. Both are randomly assigned.
  5. Players keep the power card anonymous as they may lie about what it does to other players.
  6. Once everyone is registered, normal play begins.

Core Rules

  • Players cannot run their own programs, but they can run the programs of any other player with that player's agreement.
  • Players may lie about what program they are handing over.
  • To run a program, go to the Terminal, identify yourself with your player registration card, then show the acquired power card and follow the on-screen prompts.
  • Players may not target themselves with any used power unless a rule explicitly says otherwise.
  • A player may hold only one other player’s power card at a time.
  • A received power card may not be passed on to a third player.
  • After receiving another player's power card, the player must go to the Terminal immediately, or resolve it as soon as the Terminal is free.
  • A player may not accept another player's power card with the intention of not using it.
  • After visiting the Terminal, the executor must immediately return the power card to its owner.
  • An executor may not run the same program twice in a row.

What to find out

  • Other players' roles so you can understand their powers.
  • Other players' factions so you know who is on your team.
  • Who is the Architect so you can stop them from scoring or move that state to someone in your faction.
  • Trace-log outputs become corrupted and cannot be trusted while the executor is corrupted.
  • Where the corruption currently is.

Corruption

  • Corruption is a state of a player that is placed upon them by another player
  • Corruption gives false information to the corrupted executor.
  • Yes becomes No, and No becomes Yes, where possible.
  • Trace-log outputs become corrupted and cannot be trusted while the executor is corrupted.
  • A player is not told if they are corrupted when using a program unless there is a specific power in effect.
  • If a corrupted Architect installs a new Grid effect, the point for that install goes to the other faction.

Game Loop

  1. Players talk, bluff, negotiate, and hand power cards to each other.
  2. The receiving player, the executor, walks to the Terminal and identifies themselves, or waits until the Terminal is free.
  3. The executor scans their registration card and then the power card at the Terminal.
  4. The Terminal prompts for any targets or selections and resolves the real program.
  5. If the executor is currently an Architect, they may install one Grid effect and gain a point for their faction.

Architect Install Rule

An Architect cannot use the same power twice to install a new Grid effect. But they may use other players' power cards in line with the normal rules of the game.

Example: if an Architect installs using a Registry power card, their future installs must come from a different power or a different Architect could use the same power.

Winning

Every time an Architect installs an effect, that Architect’s faction scores 1 point (unless the Architect is corrupted). The first faction to hit the session victory threshold wins immediately.

Typical thresholds: 4 points for 6-7 players, 5 points for 8-10 players, 6 points for 11-12 players.
